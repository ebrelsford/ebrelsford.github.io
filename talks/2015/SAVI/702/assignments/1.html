Make your first maps with QGIS

In class this week we covered cartographic design principles and got a taste of QGIS. Use this assignment to get more familiar with QGIS and use it to compose printable maps.

Part 1: A Small Scale Map

Pick a part of the world that you like or want to know more about. Download Natural Earth data to make a map of the region you chose. Natural Earth is a free-to-use and accurate data set for the whole world, and it is the go-to data for this type of task. You should be working at a smaller scale (that is, zoomed out), so you will likely be interested in the 10m cultural and 10m physical vector files, but feel free to use any that make sense for your map. All of the files are available on the Natural Earth downloads page.

Part 2: A Map of NYC

Make a map of something in NYC by downloading some data (eg from the NYC Open Data Portal) and mapping it with QGIS.

Finding data can be difficult, and we will be going over this in more detail in future classes. I suggest that you start looking for data early and email the mailing list if you have specific data you are looking for and are having a hard time finding. To give you an idea of what's out there, some data sets I find entertaining / useful are 311 calls, Graffiti Locations, and Wifi hotspots.

You need to find data that has coordinates--likely latitude and longitude or state plane Long Island. We will be going over how to turn data with addresses into spatial data (geocoding), but you're not expected to do this in this assignment. To find data: filter the datasets to view only maps, select a dataset, find the Export button near the top right of the page, and select Shapefile.

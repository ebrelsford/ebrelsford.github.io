Assignment 4

Readings

We are going to be getting into making web pages in the next few weeks. Here are some recommended readings to prepare you:

  • What is Code? This is a long, at times hilarious, article that was in Bloomberg Businessweek last summer. As such, it's meant for a general audience and does an interesting job of introducing readers to programming and programmers. I strongly recommend at least reading a bit of it.
  • Start to get used to HTML by reading Mozilla's Introduction to HTML. It's okay if you're not completely comfortable with this material yet, we will talk about it more next week.
  • If you're interested in working ahead a bit, start working on Codecademy's HTML & CSS course.

Part 1: Spatial SQL

Let's take another look at SQL:

  1. Download the most recent Airbnb data for NYC from Inside Airbnb. Upload it to your CartoDB account.
  2. Download the City Council Districts from the NYC OpenData portal. Upload it to your CartoDB account.
  3. Write an SQL query that selects only the Airbnb listings in a City Council District of your choosing and maps them.

Part 2: Putting things online (Neocities)

We are going to be putting web pages online starting next week. Let's create a few accounts that will lay the groundwork for that:

  1. Create a free account at Neocities.
  2. Create a simple site. You do not need to touch the HTML for now.
  3. Upload an image that you don't mind making public to your site.
  4. Find the public link to the image by clicking on it.

Part 3: Putting things online (GitHub Pages)

GitHub is another free way to host pages online. Learn a bit more about them with this tutorial. Specifically follow along starting at the heading Setting Up Your First Page:

  1. Create an account at GitHub.
  2. Create a repository as specified in the tutorial. The name should be yourusername.github.io.
  3. In the tutorial, the location of the Settings button has changed to be near the top of the page.
  4. Pick a layout.
  5. You can stop when you get to the heading Editing Your First Page.

Part 4: Make a map related to your project

In CartoDB, make a map that is related to one of your project ideas. Spend some time looking for data. Focus specifically on layering data and potential SQL queries that make interesting visualizations of the data. Don't forget legends, titles, and attribution where appropriate.

Submitting your work

Part 1: Submit your SQL query and a screenshot of the result.

Part 2: Submit the public link to the image.

Part 3: Submit the public link to your page (yourusername.github.io).

Part 4: Submit a link to your map.

You may optionally choose to include a short paragraph response or summary of your work.
